Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter 1
  • As the novel opens, an unknown, aging black woman walks down a dirt road into a town known as Eatonville as a few citizens watch from a nearby porch belonging to Pheoby Watson.
  • The citizens look on smugly as they know the woman and despise her beauty.
  • They discus her previous departure with a younger man by the name of Tea Cake, and speculate that perhaps he took her money and left her.
  • The reader comes to learn that the woman is Janie Starks.
  • Meanwhile on the porch Pheoby becomes upset with the other women and berates them for gossiping about Janie.
  • She then proceeds to head towards Janie's house to greet her and bring her food.
  • When Pheoby arrives at Janie's home she tells her of the women's speculations, however Janie explains that they are entirely wrong, she instead left the Everglades because she was no longer happy.
  • Janie then proceeds to tell the story of her experience with Tea Cake to Pheoby.
  • Chapter 2
  • Before Janie goes into details about her life in the Everglades, she decides to start from the very beginning of her life.
  • Janie explains that when she was growing up she was raised by her grandmother, Nanny, after she was left by her mother.
  • Janie and Nanny lived out behind a white family's home in a small house.
  • The family they lived with was the Washburn family, and Janie grew up playing with their children.
  • As a child Janie initially believed she was white until she was shown a photograph of herself.
  • During her time at school, Janie was often teased for both her skin color, and for living behind a white family's home.
  • The students also tease her for being left by her parents and call her father a criminal.
  • After a few years Nanny eventually saves up enough to move her and Janie to another house on their own land.
  • The story quickly shifts to when Janie is a sixteen year old girl.
  • Janie had begun to discover her sexuality and developed an attraction for a local boy, Johnny Taylor.
  • One day Janie and the Taylor boy are seen kissing by Nanny who begins to fear that this will lead to more and eventually Janie will run away with him.
  • Nanny's fear prompts her to arrange a marriage between Janie and a middle-aged farmer, Logan Killicks.
  • Nanny feels that Logan will provide Janie with security which eases Nanny's fears and comforts her in her old age.
  • Janie is upset by the marriage, however Nanny explains that she is only doing this out of her best interests and tells Janie of her struggles growing up.
  • Nanny was born into slavery and as a young woman was raped by her owner.
  • The experience left Nanny with a child, Leafy, and also put her in danger on her farm as the slave owner's wife realized that Leafy was the owner's daughter.
  • Nanny was able to escape with her daughter and started working for the Washburns.
  • Nanny hoped for a better life for her daughter, however after Leafy is raped by her school teacher and gives birth to Janie she begins to rely on alcohol and eventually runs away.
  • Nanny raises Janie as her own daughter.
  • Chapter 3
  • Janie begins to accept the upcoming wedding, however she does not develop any feelings for Logan before they are married.
  • She convinces herself that feelings will come, however after more than two months of marriage she still struggles to find feelings.
  • Janie decides to meet with Nanny to discuss this and Nanny criticizes her for complaining.
  • Nanny tells her that she should appreciate being married to a man of Logan's status and tells her to return to him and that love will eventually come.
  • A month later Nanny falls ill and dies, and many months after that Janie begins to lose hope of ever developing feelings for Logan.
  • Chapter 4
  • As the relationship wears on Janie is treated less like Logan's wife and more like another one of his laborers.
  • Logan even plans to buy a mule for Janie to help him in the fields.
  • Janie is so disillusioned with the marriage that one day while left alone she happens to see a man walking past the farm and she approaches him and begins to flirt.
  • The man is Joe Starks and he is on his way to Florida to start a new life in a new town.
  • The town is run entirely by blacks, and Joe has ambitious plans for his role in the community.
  • The two flirt for a few weeks and Janie begins to develop feelings for Joe.
  • They eventually become so close that Joe asks her to run away with him.
  • Janie is unsure at first, however after a series of fights with Logan, Janie packs up and runs away with Joe.
  • The two begin their move and get married along the way.
  • Joe becomes known as Jody from this chapter on out as Janie has created this nickname for him.
  • Chapter 5
  • They eventually arrive in the new town and Jody learns that it is named Eatonville.
  • He begins to question a few men he meets about the town and learns that not only is there no mayor, it is only 50 acres.
  • Jody is shocked by its small size and immediately purchases another 200 acres with cash from a man named Captain Eaton.
  • Eaton had donated the 50 acres initially.
  • It is immediately clear to the people already in Eatonville that Jody is a very charismatic and ambitious man.
  • Jody is very quick in creating Eatoville into a legitimate town.
  • He hires two men, Lee Coker and Tony Taylor, to build him a store and post office, he then hires other members of the town to help develop roads.
  • Jody also sets out to recruit new people to the town and sells plots of land immediately making back the money he spent on the 200 acres.
  • Jody's work in Eatonville gets him promoted to town mayor and a ceremony is held for him on the porch of his new store.
  • At the ceremony Janie is called on by Tony Taylor to give a speech about Jody, however Jody does not allow this which upsets Janie greatly.
  • Jody's next order of action is to buy a street lamp for the town which is a symbol of great pride for the citizens and a large ceremony is held for the lighting of it.
  • Following the ceremony Janie tells Jody that she hopes they can settle down for a while after all his hard work, however Jody has no intentions of this.
  • Overtime Jody becomes less esteemed in the town and Janie becomes an object of envy to the other women.
  • Jody builds a very large house with lavish accessories and it is quite clear to everyone else that not only is he better off, he knows it.
  • The citizens begin to resent him, however no one ever challenges him.
  • Janie also begins to feel oppressed by him as he is very controlling of her.
  • Chapter 6
  • Janie is required by Jody to run his store, a job she finds very tedious and dissatisfying.
  • Janie does however enjoy one aspect of her job, the interaction with the customers.
  • Many of them will often sit on the porch outside and socialize which Janie enjoys.
  • Jody however forbids Janie from sitting with those on the porch as he sees them as trash.
  • Janie is very upset with his treatment of her and he also forces her to wear a rag over her hair while she works in order to hide her beautiful hair.
  • One of the often discussed topics on the porch is about a weak mule that is owned by a man named Matt Bonner.
  • The mule escapes from Bonner's care one day and shows up at the store causing some of the men on the porch to mess with it.
  • Janie is bothered by this and voices her disappointment to herself, however she is overheard by Jody.
  • Jody is inspired by Janie's feelings towards the mule and purchases it from Bonner so it can live happily in its old age.
  • Jody is seen as very noble for this act.
  • Eventually the mule succumbs to its age and dies causing Jody to hold a funeral for it as the mule had become quite an important symbol in the town.
  • Jody however forbids Janie from attending the funeral which is the cause for an argument between the two later at the store.
  • Janie, though she disagrees with Jody and his beliefs about gender roles, decides to give up her argument and Jody heads outside to enjoy the company of the other men on the porch.
  • Janie spends some time outside with the group as they make playful jokes to one another, however after a while Jody scolds her for being out of place and sends her inside.
  • Later on back in the store Janie makes a mistake causing Jody to react harshly towards her.
  • Janie continues to be silent to Jody's insults and demands, however she begins to build up a feeling of resentment towards him.
  • She is unable to keep her emotions inside indefinitely.
  • The following day some of the men gossip about Tony Robbins' wife.
  • They criticize her actions, as she had previously begged Jody for extra meat in the store, and call her an embarrassment to herself and her husband.
  • Janie stands up for her and exclaims that it is very difficult for women, however Jody is very upset by this and sends her away to fetch a checkerboard.
  • Chapter 7
  • Over the next few years Janie gives in to Jody's oppression and begins to suppress all of her emotions.
  • She accepts her fate and believes that she will live the rest of her life submitting to Jody's will.
  • However after some years of these feelings Janie begins to notice that Jody has begun to look very ill.
  • Jody is aging and deteriorating very quickly, and with this deterioration comes many harsh verbal attacks on Janie.
  • One day Jody lashes out at her after a mistake in the store and embarrasses her in front of several customers.
  • Janie finally stands up for herself after years of submissiveness and Jody is so shocked he strikes her causing her to flee from the store.
  • Chapter 8
  • Jody spends the next few months living in a separate room from Janie as his health deteriorates quickly.
  • He searches for help, however no one seems to offer any advice.
  • Eventually it becomes a rumor in town that Janie has been poisoning Jody, which causes Janie to find a real doctor to treat Jody.
  • The doctor examines Jody and says that his kidneys are failing and that he does not have long to live.
  • Janie decides that she wants to face Jody one last time before he dies.
  • She enters his room and the two have a discussion that quickly escalates into an argument as Janie expresses all her pent up emotions towards Jody.
  • She berates his treatment of her and tells him that he is dying.
  • Jody becomes very emotional, and succumbs to death as they argue.
  • Janie feels liberated and rips off her hair cover, however she realizes that it will appear as if she caused his death if she does not mourn.
  • She then puts on a mask of sadness and exclaims to the others that Jody has died.
  • Chapter 9
  • A lavish funeral is held for Jody, and following Janie goes through a proper stage of mourning, however deep down she is extremely relieved and joyful to be rid of Jody.
  • Janie spends six months appearing to be mourning as is custom during this time period, however when the time is up she is quick to alter her appearance to those around her.
  • Janie is soon flooded by men trying to woo her as she is not only single but still very attractive and wealthy.
  • She enjoys being free and the men that throw themselves at her, however she is not willing to settle down.
  • She wears white to show that she is ready for another man, however she has no intentions whatsoever.
  • Janie still continues to run the store, however this frustrates her as she still feels Jody's presence in doing so.
  • Despite gossip in the town that threatens Janie's reputation, she is not willing to hide her happiness about being single.
  • Chapter 10
  • One evening Janie is working with Hezekiah Potts.
  • Hezekiah decides to leave the store early that evening to attend a baseball game leaving Janie behind.
  • She plans to close up, however before she does a man she has never seen before enters the store.
  • He is very handsome and begins to flirt with Janie.
  • He then asks Janie to join him in a game of checkers which flatters Janie as she has never been respected enough by a man to play checkers.
  • The two flirt over the game of checkers and begin to learn a bit about each other.
  • Janie learns that the man's name is Vergible Woods, however he goes by Tea Cake.
  • The two continue to flirt for many hours until the store is again filled with customers following the game.
  • Tea Cake however sticks around even after this and helps Janie close up the store after everyone leaves.
  • He walks her back to her home and the two say goodnight.
  • Chapter 11
  • It is over a week before Tea Cake returns to the store to visit Janie.
  • In the meantime, Janie convinces herself that he is only interested in her wealth.
  • She plans to be very rude to him on his next visit so as to prevent being taken advantage of, however when he does finally turn up again she is taken by his flirtatious ways and forgets all about her vows.
  • The two spend the evening much like the last time they were together, playing checkers and talking for hours.
  • Tea Cake eventually walks Janie home and the two spend more time together talking on Janie's porch.
  • After a long time of talking Tea Cake asks Janie if she would like to go fishing, and the two spend nearly the rest of the night down by the lake enjoying each others' company.
  • The following day while Janie speaks with Hezekiah Potts, Hezekiah tells her that she should avoid Tea Cake as he is a man of very low status.
  • Janie ignores this and spends that evening with Tea Cake as well.
  • The two have dinner, and following dinner, they lounge on the couch for hours and Janie eventually falls asleep on Tea Cake's lap.
  • When she awakes the two discuss the relationship they have.
  • Tea Cake is afraid that Janie sees him as an impostor.
  • She explains that is entirely false, however she sees him as just a friend.
  • Tea Cake is devastated as he explains that he sees her as much more than that.
  • Janie cannot see why as she is much older and tells Tea Cake that his feelings will soon pass.
  • Tea Cake leaves following this and it is two days before he returns again.
  • When he does return he tells Janie that he means everything he said about his feelings, and later that night the two have dinner again and spend the night together.
  • When he leaves the next morning, he doesn't return for three days, which worries Janie greatly, however, when he does return he comes with a great surprise.
  • Tea Cake arrives and tells Janie that he wants to take their relationship public, he has also bought an old, beat up car, and plans to drive to the town picnic in it with Janie and let everyone know where the two stand.
  • Chapter 12
  • Following the picnic and Janie and Tea Cake's announcement, the two become the center of controversy in the town.
  • Everyone begins speaking about their romance and cannot believe that someone of Janie's status would see such a man.
  • Pheoby and Sam Watson discus the relationship and Sam convinces her to talk to Janie and try to convince her against dating Tea Cake.
  • Pheoby speaks with Janie and tells her that Tea Cake is far too low of a man for her to be seen with and suggests that he will just take advantage of her money and leave her.
  • Janie does not want to hear any of this as she sees Tea Cake as being much more than that, she explains that she plans to sell everything and leave town with Tea Cake.
  • Pheoby gives one last push, however it is to no avail and the two celebrate Janie's new happiness.
  • Chapter 13
  • Janie packs up and leaves soon after, she then meets up in Jacksonville, Florida with Tea Cake where the two marry.
  • Janie has hidden two hundred dollars inside her shirt, which she plans to keep a secret from Tea Cake in case he does try to take advantage of her.
  • Sure enough, a week later Tea Cake goes missing with Janie's money.
  • Janie thinks the worst, however after a while Tea Cake returns.
  • He explains that when he saw the money he was so excited he decided to spend it on a feast of chicken and macaroni for a few men he works with.
  • He would have brought along Janie, however the get together got out of hand and he would have been embarrassed to show his friends to Janie.
  • Tea Cake feels bad for taking her money and promises to make it back by gambling.
  • He then leaves that weekend and Janie again worries that he will not return, however he shows up with over three hundred dollars that he won, as well as a few cuts and bruises from some sore losers.
  • Janie finally begins to trust him and tells him about some twelve hundred dollars she has saved, however he says that she will not need it.
  • He adds that he will take care of her, and the two can move to the Everglades and live a simple life.
  • Chapter 14
  • Janie is overwhelmed by her feelings for Tea Cake as for the first time she has found someone she really cares for.
  • The two settle into life in the Everglades and Tea Cake begins to show her the way of life.
  • They spend the days planting beans, and the evenings relaxing, playing dice, and enjoying each others’ company.
  • After a short while many migrant workers move into the area and work begins to pick up.
  • At first Janie spends the days cooking while Tea Cake works, however she misses spending time with him and joins him working in the swamps.
  • Their home becomes a gathering place for the community and she has never been happier with her life.
  • She thinks back to the people in Eatonville and what they would think if they saw her now.
  • Chapter 15
  • Out in the fields Tea Cake works with a girl named Nunkie.
  • She is a younger, chunky girl that flirts with Tea Cake.
  • As the planting season wears on, Nunkie begins to flirt with Tea Cake even more, causing Janie to become very jealous.
  • One day while working Janie notices that Nunkie and Tea Cake have disappeared, when she goes to look for them she finds them wrestling playfully.
  • Janie is furious and drags Tea Cake home where she tries to beat him, however it soon turns into passionate romance.
  • The two wake up the following day with the whole incident behind them.
  • Chapter 16
  • Eventually the planting season ends, however Tea Cake and Janie decide to stick around during the offseason unlike the migrant workers.
  • Being that their is plenty of off time Janie becomes very social and begins making friends with many of the others that live year round in the Everglades.
  • One friend of Janie's is Mrs. Turner, she is an older black woman, however she spends much of her time complaining about blacks.
  • She believes blacks are a lazy population and she admires whites.
  • Mrs. Turner also judges blacks on the tone of their skin, for example she despises and fears Tea Cake, as he is very dark skinned.
  • She suggests to Janie that she leave him for her brother who is much lighter skinned.
  • Tea Cake does not approve of the friendship between Janie and Mrs. Turner, and he plans to approach Mr. Turner and ask him to keep his wife away from their home.
  • However, when Tea Cake comes across Mr. Turner he finds him to be a very weary and run-down man as Mrs. Turner is a very controlling wife.
  • In the end Tea Cake doesn't approach Mr. Turner, and Mrs. Turner continues to show up to socialize with Janie despite Janie's cold attitude towards her.
  • In the end not much comes from this and the offseason is short lived as the planting season begins again.
  • Chapter 17
  • The new season begins, and with it comes many new and old migrant workers.
  • One of the new workers is Mrs. Turner's brother who is a handsome man.
  • Tea Cake becomes very jealous of him and beats Janie as a result.
  • Tea Cake is very ashamed of beating her however, and for a long time after that pampers her.
  • Janie seems to forget the beating almost instantly and every man in the community becomes very jealous for Tea Cake for his ability to control and manipulate Janie.
  • During the planting season many of the workers spend Saturday afternoons and evenings drinking, as it is payday.
  • One particular Saturday two men, Coode May and Dick Sterrett become obnoxiously drunk and enter Mrs. Turner's restaurant where Tea Cake and some of his friends are eating.
  • Tea Cake, trying to impress Mrs. Turner, steps in and tries to kick the men out of the restaurant, however a ruckus ensues.
  • The restaurant is trashed, Mrs. Turner is injured, and Tea Cake's plan backfires.
  • Chapter 18
  • Strange happenings begin to occur in the Everglades as one-day Janie notices many natives seem to be picking up and leaving town almost suddenly.
  • Janie inquires into their departures and learns that a hurricane is approaching.
  • Over the next few days several other people leave, however a select few decide to stay behind and wait it out, two of them being Janie and Tea Cake.
  • At first a group of those staying take refuge at Tea Cake's home, however as the storm becomes close everyone but a man, Motor Boat, leave for their homes.
  • As the storm brews Tea Cake remarks that Janie probably wishes she was back home in Eatonville, however she states that she is happiest with Tea Cake.
  • The storm continues to turn worse and soon severe flooding begins to threaten their home causing them and Motor Boat to flee.
  • Over the next several hours the three wade through rising waters before stopping at a house on higher ground.
  • Motor Boat decides to stay behind, however Janie and Tea Cake continue on.
  • Their travels take them across treacherous, flooded land, where bodies float and homes are destroyed.
  • At one point while wading through the water Janie spots a cow walking with a dog resting on its back.
  • Janie tries to use the cow as a way to stay out of the water, however the rabid dog attacks, and Tea Cake must step in to save her.
  • Tea Cake is bit in the scuffle, however he manages to kill the dog and the two continue on before stopping in Palm Beach to rest again.
  • Chapter 19
  • In Palm Beach it is clear to the two that they are no better off as many have died there as well.
  • Tea Cake is even forced at gunpoint by two white men to help bury corpses.
  • He is so disgusted and appalled by his work that the two decide to return to the Everglades.
  • Back in the Everglades they discover that many of their friends have died, however many have survived as well, including Motor Boat.
  • Tea Cake helps in the effort to rebuild the town, however after a few weeks he begins to seem very sick and different.
  • Tea Cake gets terribly ill as he struggles to eat and is very irritable, Janie summons a doctor who pulls her aside and tells her that he believes Tea Cake is suffering from the dog bite.
  • The doctor believes it is too late to save Tea Cake, however he offers to order medicine anyways to see if it will help.
  • Tea Cake grows deranged by the rabies, and it is clear to Janie that he is dangerous, he also begins to sleep with a pistol under his pillow.
  • Janie hopes his craziness will pass, however to make sure she is safe she takes his pistol and moves the three bullets inside the clip back three spots so that he has to pull the trigger three times before actually shooting.
  • She hopes that if he attempts anything she will have time to react.
  • Sure enough one afternoon Tea Cake begins making accusations against Janie, and threatens her with the gun.
  • As he goes through the three trigger pulls Janie knows that she has no other options and pulls out a rifle and shoots Tea Cake.
  • She is immediately arrested and put on trial, however after her testimony she is released as there is no evidence of wrong-doing on her part.
  • Janie decides to give Tea Cake a proper burial following her trial.
  • Chapter 20
  • The funeral ends and with the ending comes an outpouring of sympathy for Janie by others in the town, particularly the men who had treated her badly.
  • As an act of retribution, a group of men run Mrs. Turner's brother out of town.
  • Despite the new support from others in the Everglades, Janie sees that their is nothing left for her there and she decides to pack up and leave.
  • Her story finally concludes and it shifts back to Janie and Pheoby's conversation.
  • Janie tells Pheoby that she has no regrets, and that despite the gossip that is sure to face her in Eatonville, she is willing to put it aside and live out her life there.
  • That evening while trying to sleep Janie thinks about the ending of Tea Cake's life, yet she puts it in perspective with the rest of the time they spent together and realizes that she lived a blessed life with him.
