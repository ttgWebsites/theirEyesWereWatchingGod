Characters

  • Janie Crawford
  • Janie is the protagonist of the novel.
  • She is described as a black woman with straight hair much like a white person, which she proceeds to flaunt throughout her life.
  • Janie spends much of her life trying to defy stereotypes of both her race and gender.
  • She proves to be a very mature and self reliant individual.
  • Nanny Crawford
  • Nanny is Janie's grandmother and raises her following Janie's mother's departure.
  • Nanny desires a life of security and happiness for her granddaughter and arranges a marriage for her with Logan.
  • Leafy Crawford
  • Leafy is Janie's mother, she becomes pregnant with Janie after being raped, and abandons her shortly after she is born.
  • Tea Cake
  • Tea Cake is Janie's third husband, and proves to be Janie's first and only real love.
  • Tea Cake is a very wise and quick witted man, and he is also very affectionate towards Janie.
  • Though Tea Cake beats Janie on occasion, he proves up until the end that he deeply cares for her.
  • Logan Killicks
  • Janie's first husband by arranged marriage, he seems to care for Janie for the beginning of the relationship, however after a while begins to treat her as if she works for him.
  • Janie eventually runs away from him with another man.
  • Jody Starks
  • Jody is Janie's second husband.
  • He is very obsessed with power and spends his life trying to improve his position both politically and materially.
  • His relationship with Janie is not very satisfying for either as he wants her to be his trophy wife, and she desires a relationship founded on love, however neither really occurs.
  • Amos Hicks
  • He is the first resident of Eatonville to meet Jody and Janie, he also tries to woo Janie, however she wants nothing of it.
  • Pheoby Watson
  • Pheoby becomes Janie's best friend during their time in Eatonville.
  • When Janie moves back following her marriage with Tea Cake, Pheoby is the only person close to Janie and listens to her as she tells her life story.
  • Sam Watson
  • Sam is Pheoby's husband, and though he plays a minor role in the novel he is a significant figure on Jody's porch at the general store.
  • The Turners
  • Mr. and Mrs. Turner are a couple who own a restaurant in the Everglades.
  • Mrs. Turner gets along greatly with Janie as she recognizes her Caucasian features, however she struggles to understand why she would marry Tea Cake.
  • Motor Boat
  • Motor Boat is one of Janie and Tea Cake's friends in the Everglades and when the hurricane strikes, he weathers the storm with them.
  • Johnny Taylor
  • The first boy that Janie shows any affection towards, however this prompts Nanny to marry Janie away.
  • Nunkie
  • Nunkie flirts with Tea Cake while in the Everglades, however he denies any relations with her and Janie believes him.
