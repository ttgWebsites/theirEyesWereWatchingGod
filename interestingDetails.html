Interesting Details

  • When the novel was published in the first half of the 20th century it was poorly received by many. It was far ahead of its time having a black-female protagonist. It wasn't until after the civil rights movement when it became a widely read piece of literature.
  • In 2005 Oprah Winfrey was the executive producer of a movie version of the novel. The movie however downplayed the issues of race and religion, and instead decided to focus more on romance.
  • Hurston grew up in the town of Eatonville, Florida, the town, which plays a significant role in the novel. Her father was even mayor of the town; the protagonist's husband in the novel is mayor of Eatonville.
